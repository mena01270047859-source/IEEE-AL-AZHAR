What is IEEE Al-Azhar?

IEEE Al-Azhar is a student branch of the global IEEE organization, created to support students in technology, engineering, programming, and scientific research. The branch provides a learning environment that helps students develop both practical and theoretical skills through workshops, projects, competitions, and technical events. It also helps students build a strong network with people in the same field and gain experience similar to real work environments. IEEE encourages innovation, teamwork, and personal development, offering students valuable opportunities to grow and improve their skills before graduation.

What do students learn in the Front-End field at IEEE Al-Azhar?

In the Front-End field at IEEE Al-Azhar, students learn the fundamentals of building website interfaces using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. They then move on to advanced concepts such as interface design, writing clean and organized code, and working with modern frameworks like React. The committee also focuses on hands-on training through real projects that teach students how to turn designs into interactive web pages. Along with UI/UX principles, students develop teamwork, time management, and project-based work skills, preparing them for internships and real job opportunities.
